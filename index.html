Contrasting Perspectives on Retirement in Hong Kong .
香港退休觀念的對比視角。
In a rapidly evolving social landscape, the way individuals perceive retirement has taken center stage, as a recent survey unveils starkly contrasting views between different generations.
在一個急速變遷的社會環境中，個人如何看待退休已成為焦點，一項近期調查揭示了不同世代之間截然不同的觀點。
While the majority of people in their 60s express an inclination to extend their working lives beyond the traditional retirement age of 65, a noteworthy surge in young people aiming to retire in their 30s or 40s highlights the shifting paradigms surrounding retirement.
雖然大多數六十多歲的人士表示傾向於將工作生涯延長至傳統的六十五歲退休年齡之後，但值得關注的是，目標在三十多或四十多歲退休的年輕人數目顯著上升，突顯了圍繞退休的觀念範式正在轉變。
The survey conducted by the Financial Planning Research Group, involving a diverse cross-section of Hong Kong’s population, brings to light the complex attitudes towards retirement.
由財務策劃研究組進行的這項調查，涵蓋了香港社會各階層人士，揭示了對退休的複雜態度。
According to the findings, more than 70% of respondents in their 60s expressed a desire to continue working past the age of 65.
調查結果顯示，超過七成六十多歲的受訪者表示希望在六十五歲後繼續工作。
At the top of their list of priorities is financial stability.
他們優先考慮清單上首位是財務穩定。
As the first cohort to navigate through major economic events such as the financial crisis in 1997, the SARS outbreak in 2003, the financial tsunami in 2008 and the global pandemic in 2019, they recognize the vital role continued employment plays in sustaining their quality of life.
作為首批經歷了1997年金融危機、2003年沙士爆發、2008年金融海嘯及2019年全球疫情等重大經濟事件的一代，他們認識到持續就業對於維持生活質素起著至關重要的作用。
Over the past few decades, economic uncertainty and the relentless rise in the cost of living have compelled them to remain engaged in the workforce.
過去幾十年，經濟的不確定性及生活成本的不斷上升，迫使他們必須保持參與勞動力市場。
Accordingly, they perceive working as the best way to ensure their ability to afford the necessities and even the luxuries in their later years.
因此，他們視工作為確保晚年有能力負擔生活必需品甚至奢侈品的最佳方式。
Another factor that fuels the desire of this generation to keep working is the sense of purpose that employment provides.
驅使這一代人希望繼續工作的另一個因素，是工作所提供的意義感。
Work is not just about earning a paycheck; it encompasses a sense of identity, routine and social interaction.
工作不僅僅是為了賺取薪金；它還包含身份認同感、生活規律和社交互動。
The daily routines they've built over the years, the camaraderie they've formed with their co-workers and the meaningful tasks they perform in their jobs all come together to give them a sense of purpose.
他們多年來建立的日常規律、與同事建立的友誼，以及工作中所執行的有意義任務，共同賦予他們一種目標感。
Many older individuals actually derive fulfillment from their professional roles and find purpose in their contributions to their fields.
許多年長者實際上從他們的專業角色中獲得滿足感，並在對其所屬領域的貢獻中找到人生意義。
Retirement, for them, could signify missing out on these things – the mental challenges, the chance to talk to friends at work and the sense of accomplishment.
對他們而言，退休可能意味著錯失這些東西——心智上的挑戰、與工作朋友交談的機會，以及成就感。
In stark contrast to the older generation’s perspectives, the younger demographic is redefining their views on retirement.
與老一輩的觀點形成鮮明對比的是，年輕一代正在重新定義他們對退休的看法。
Under the influence of the FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement, these millennials are driven to embrace an entirely different approach to work and financial strategizing.
在財務自主，提早退休（FIRE）運動的影響下，這些千禧一代被驅使去接納一種完全不同的工作和財務策略方式。
Central to this movement is the principle of financial independence, which encourages people to save aggressively, invest wisely from a young age and live frugally to amass enough wealth to retire decades ahead of the traditional age.
此運動的核心原則是財務獨立，鼓勵人們積極儲蓄、從小明智投資並節儉生活，以積累足夠財富，比傳統退休年齡提前數十年退休。
With some luck, retiring in one’s 30s or 40s is no longer an unachievable dream.
若有些許運氣，在三十多或四十多歲退休已不再是無法實現的夢想。
Moreover, the pursuit of passions plays a pivotal role in the aspirations of the young generation towards early retirement.
此外，對熱情的追求在年輕一代嚮往提早退休的願望中扮演關鍵角色。
The desire to explore personal interests, travel and immerse themselves in life experiences outside work further motivates them to achieve financial independence as early as possible.
探索個人興趣、旅行以及投入工作以外生活體驗的渴望，進一步激勵他們盡早實現財務獨立。
For them, retirement means a newfound freedom to pursue their dreams and aspirations without being constrained by the demands of traditional employment which, more often than not, confines a worker to an office cubicle for as long as 10 hours from Monday to Friday.
對他們來說，退休意味著一種新獲得的自由，可以追求夢想和抱負，而不受傳統僱傭要求的束縛——這些要求往往將工作者困在辦公室格子間裡，每週一至週五長達十小時。
Once they have accumulated enough money to support themselves, they simply want to live life on their terms and create meaningful memories outside of their regular jobs.
一旦積累了足夠資金維生，他們便只想按照自己的方式生活，並在常規工作之外創造有意義的回憶。
As Hong Kong adapts to the progressively diverging views on retirement, it becomes paramount to foster open dialogues between the older and younger generations.
隨著香港適應日益分歧的退休觀念，促進老一輩與年輕一代之間的開放對話變得至關重要。
Policymakers should heed these differing desires in an aim to create flexible retirement plans that cater to both seniors seeking meaningful employment and young enthusiasts striving for early independence.
政策制定者應重視這些不同的願望，旨在制定靈活的退休計劃，以同時滿足尋求有意義工作的年長者，以及爭取提早獨立的年輕熱衷者。

Contrasting Perspectives on Retirement in Hong Kong

In a rapidly evolving social landscape, the way individuals perceive retirement has taken center stage, as a recent survey unveils starkly contrasting views between different generations. While the majority of people in their 60s express an inclination to extend their working lives beyond the traditional retirement age of 65, a noteworthy surge in young people aiming to retire in their 30s or 40s highlights the shifting paradigms surrounding retirement.
The survey conducted by the Financial Planning Research Group, involving a diverse cross-section of Hong Kong’s population, brings to light the complex attitudes towards retirement. According to the findings, more than 70% of respondents in their 60s expressed a desire to continue working past the age of 65. At the top of their list of priorities is financial stability. As the first cohort to navigate through major economic events such as the financial crisis in 1997, the SARS outbreak in 2003, the financial tsunami in 2008 and the global pandemic in 2019, they recognize the vital role continued employment plays in sustaining their quality of life. Over the past few decades, economic uncertainty and the relentless rise in the cost of living have compelled them to remain engaged in the workforce. Accordingly, they perceive working as the best way to ensure their ability to afford the necessities and even the luxuries in their later years.
Another factor that fuels the desire of this generation to keep working is the sense of purpose that employment provides. Work is not just about earning a paycheck; it encompasses a sense of identity, routine and social interaction. The daily routines they've built over the years, the camaraderie they've formed with their co-workers and the meaningful tasks they perform in their jobs all come together to give them a sense of purpose. Many older individuals actually derive fulfillment from their professional roles and find purpose in their contributions to their fields. Retirement, for them, could signify missing out on these things – the mental challenges, the chance to talk to friends at work and the sense of accomplishment.
In stark contrast to the older generation’s perspectives, the younger demographic is redefining their views on retirement. Under the influence of the FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement, these millennials are driven to embrace an entirely different approach to work and financial strategizing. Central to this movement is the principle of financial independence, which encourages people to save aggressively, invest wisely from a young age and live frugally to amass enough wealth to retire decades ahead of the traditional age. With some luck, retiring in one’s 30s or 40s is no longer an unachievable dream.
Moreover, the pursuit of passions plays a pivotal role in the aspirations of the young generation towards early retirement. The desire to explore personal interests, travel and immerse themselves in life experiences outside work further motivates them to achieve financial independence as early as possible. For them, retirement means a newfound freedom to pursue their dreams and aspirations without being constrained by the demands of traditional employment which, more often than not, confines a worker to an office cubicle for as long as 10 hours from Monday to Friday. Once they have accumulated enough money to support themselves, they simply want to live life on their terms and create meaningful memories outside of their regular jobs.
As Hong Kong adapts to the progressively diverging views on retirement, it becomes paramount to foster open dialogues between the older and younger generations. Policymakers should heed these differing desires in an aim to create flexible retirement plans that cater to both seniors seeking meaningful employment and young enthusiasts striving for early independence.
